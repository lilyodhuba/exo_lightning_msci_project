### MSci Physics with Astrophysics final year project researching lightning on Earth-like exoplanets.

# Abstract:
In this project, we aimed to investigate the environmental conditions affecting lightning generation on Earth-like
exoplanets. 
We analyse data from the Met Office Unified Model (UM), a 3D general circulation model, to update the parameters in a 1-dimensional entraining plume model for lightning generation. 
Of the many parameters that could be varied, we investigate the surface pressure and air temperature profile by constructing dimensionless pressure-temperature (σ-T) profiles for different atmospheric and surface predictions for TRAPPIST-1e, a tidally locked exoplanet orbiting an M-dwarf star. 
From these, we created an averaged profile to update the stratospheric temperature function and the pressure level of the tropopause. We then compared the simulation outputs from using the original function, a dimensionless-scaled version, and our updated function over different surface pressures for a convective plume over the substellar point of TRAPPIST-1e. 
Our updated function produces a greater vertical extent of the lightning flash rate (LFR), predicting total power outputs greater than the original and scaled models in most cases, with a maximum excess of 1.2MW for 0.25 bar surface pressure. 
We observe no lightning activity for 2 bar surface pressure, but peak LFRs of 2.187 × 10−4 fl s−1 km−2, 9.000 × 10−5 fl s−1 km−2 and 8.814 × 10−6 fl s−1 km−2 for 0.25 bar, 0.5 bar and 1 bar respectively.
